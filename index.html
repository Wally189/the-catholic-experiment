AN ORDINARY CATHOLIC LEARNING DELIBERATELY

The Catholic Experiment

Can an ordinary working Catholic build a serious, structured education in the faith without becoming permanently attached to a screen?

I am Alan Gallagher, a Catholic convert and the learner behind this experiment. I am using books, notebooks, memory, authoritative Catholic sources and my background in technology to organise, test and document a long-term course of study.

Explore the Courses →See how it works ↓

Why I began

The Catholic Experiment began when I decided not simply to consume more information, but to return to the faith deliberately and study it in depth.

The difficulty is no longer simply finding information. It is deciding what to study, in what order, how to judge it and how to retain what has been learned.

How the Experiment works

Each Experiment begins with a serious book, course or collection of primary material. The source supplies the spine; the website supports rather than replaces study.

  • reading slowly and deliberately;
  • writing by hand;
  • copying and defining key terms;
  • completing exercises;
  • reading aloud where useful;
  • testing recall;
  • recording questions and uncertainties;
  • checking older material against authoritative modern Catholic sources;
  • reflecting honestly on what worked and what did not.

The Schools

Six Schools organise the full programme and the Experiments within it.

Faith and Formation

Doctrine, theology, moral life, prayer, liturgy and lay apostolate.

Latin, Biblical Greek, Biblical Hebrew and Aramaic.

The Bible, its history, literature, interpretation, archaeology and liturgical life.

Reason and Human Understanding

Logic, philosophy, natural theology, the human person, rhetoric and judgement.

Church and Witness

The Church, Fathers, saints, doctors, councils, history and religious life.

Church in the World

Social doctrine, canon law, governance, art, music and science.

Schedule

Only one Experiment is developed intensively at a time. Languages may run alongside the principal route where the workload remains sustainable.

Current sequence: common platform → Latin pilot definition → genuine study and delivery → evaluation → next authorised Experiment.

Materials

The book remains central. Essential learning should be possible through lawful free access, libraries or modest physical materials wherever reasonably possible.

Required

Notebook, pen or pencil, and access to the approved course text.

Recommended

A suitable dictionary, ruler, headphones and a quiet regular study period.

Optional

Flashcards, printer, loose-leaf folder or a separate vocabulary notebook.

Certificates

Certificates record completed personal study and an approved completion test. They are not academic accreditation, professional qualifications or ecclesiastical approval.
