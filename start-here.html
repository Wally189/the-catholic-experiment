What is The Catholic Experiment?

This is not a polished ministry or a parish project.

It’s one lay Catholic – mid-life, imperfect, often tired – trying to live the Catholic way of life deliberately, and sharing the story along the way.

I am based in the UK with an Irish background. I come from a non-religious home so even after 'conversion', some things - practices - can seem to be alien to me.

I've been thinking about creating these pages for quite a long time and finally decided to bite the proverbial bullet.

The idea isn't to pontificate (pun intended!); I want to share my experiences honestly, in the hope that others might find something helpful here.

I'll be clear from the outset. I am a sinner. Much as I wish that were not the case, it remains true in many areas of my life. Choosing to be a Catholic Christian means trying again, and again, and again. It's not an easy path, but it's the one I feel called to walk.

If you’re curious, drifting, slightly allergic to church politics, or wondering whether God still has patience for you: this page is where to begin. Perhaps we can walk a little way together. A pilgrimage of sorts....

“I’m not a theologian, a spiritual director or a saint. I’m just trying to live out Catholicism properly – Mass, prayer, confession, charity, study – can it actually change a life like mine? Can it change a life like yours? This is a lifelong experiment to which I am committing. Even in my darkest moments I hope and pray for the faith, hope, love and peace of Our Lord and Saviour.”

What this is (...and what it isn’t!)

Honest expectations, so you’re not misled.

This is

  • A personal spiritual project by a relatively recent convert (around two years, though complicated by attending Mass for many years before that) who still has doubts, bad days and questions.
  • A series of small “experiments” in living the Catholic life: prayer habits, sacraments, devotions, study, charity.
  • A place to speak honestly about struggle, failure, starting again and learning to forgive.
  • Loyal to the teaching of the Catholic Church, even when it’s hard, and trying to receive it as medicine rather than a weapon.
  • Especially attentive to those of us who are middle-aged seekers, “burnt” Catholics, and Catholics who still want to stay close to Christ.

This is not

  • A replacement for a parish, priest, or spiritual director - or going to Mass or taking part in the sacraments or any of those things.
  • An official project of any diocese, parish, community or religious order.
  • A place for culture-war Catholicism, online dogfights or anonymous character assassinations. If you are into the Latin Mass, good for you. If you are into the Novus Ordo, also good for you!
  • Professional counselling, therapy, or crisis support.
  • A platform where I pretend to be more sorted, holy or certain than I really am. I can vouch that I am not!

Who this is for

If any of this sounds like you, you’re in the right place.

Tired-but-faithful Catholics

You still go to Mass (mostly), but prayer feels thin, parish life wears you down, and you’re not sure where your heart has gone.

You’re not looking for drama; you just want to love God again without pretending.

Seekers & returners

You’re curious about Catholicism, or you were once Catholic and drifted. You might feel late, embarrassed, or unsure what the “rules” are now. How does being Catholic fit in the modern world?

You need a place that explains things gently, without dumbing them down or shouting.

Those carrying complicated stories

You may be LGBTQ, divorced, long away from the sacraments, or bearing wounds from Church people.

This project won’t solve everything, but it aims to hold your story with dignity while still staying close to the heart of the Church.

How to use this site

A gentle way in, without overwhelm.

1. Read a little

Begin with a few short pieces in the Reflections section, or skim the summary of an experiment that catches your eye.

You don’t need to agree with everything. Just notice what resonates and what bothers you – that’s often where God is already stirring.

2. Try one small experiment

Don’t try to “fix your whole spiritual life in a week”. Choose a single experiment – perhaps the Daily Examen, a simple confession prep, or one extra Mass – and give it a fair try.

Most experiments are designed for ordinary, messy, mid-life schedules. No monastery required!

3. Make notes

Keep a tiny notebook, a phone note, or a file where you scribble: What changed? What didn’t? What surprised me? Where did I sense God?

Over time, this becomes your own “lab notebook” of grace.

4. Repeat, rest, and be honest

Take breaks, adjust, and don’t lie to yourself about what’s working. The point is not to perform; it’s to discover how the Catholic way of life can genuinely shape you.

What I promise – and what I can’t

A few boundaries to keep everyone safe and honest.

What I promise

  • To speak as truthfully as I can about my own attempts to live the faith and my own stories.
  • To stay loyal to the Magisterium of the Catholic Church, even where and when I find it difficult.
  • To avoid gossip, character attacks, or parish politics disguised as holiness.
  • To treat stories of struggle – including around sexuality, identity, pro life issues and past wounds – with discretion and respect.
  • To keep a sense of humour. Catholics are allowed to laugh!

What I can’t offer

  • Professional mental health support or crisis intervention.
  • Authoritative answers that replace the role of a priest, confessor or spiritual director.
  • Instant replies to messages, or ongoing one-to-one accompaniment for everyone who visits.
  • A place to campaign against the Church, its teaching or its pastors.

Church loyalty & disclaimer

Independent, but not freelance Catholicism.

Loyalty: The Catholic Experiment is undertaken in good faith, in obedience to the teaching of the Catholic Church as expressed in Scripture, Tradition and the Magisterium.

I will sometimes write about tension, confusion or difficulty – including around Catholic teaching and my own life – but the aim is always to move towards deeper understanding and trust, not away from the Church.

Disclaimer: This site is a personal project. It is not an official work of any parish, diocese, religious order or Catholic organisation. Nothing here should be taken as replacing the counsel of a priest, confessor, spiritual director, or qualified professional. Views expressed are my own and are offered as personal reflection, not instruction.
