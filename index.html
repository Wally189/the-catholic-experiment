What this is

The Catholic Experiment is not a programme, a parish or a movement. It’s one person’s honest attempt to live the Catholic way of life – prayer, sacraments, study, charity – and to share what actually happens.

No perfect answers. No spiritual heroics. Just a clear-eyed journey with the Church, for those who feel a bit lost but still want God.

Who it’s for

  • Middle-aged seekers who feel late to the party
  • Catholics who are tired, angry, or quietly drifting
  • People hurt by parish politics or Church culture
  • Catholics trying to stay close to Christ
  • Anyone wondering, “Could faith actually heal my life?”

This week's experiment

Trying out one concrete Catholic practice, on purpose.

Experiment #1 · The Basics

It has been a while since I have said a prayer and meant it. That's the Gods honest truth. This week I begin by saying ONE Our Father each day, one Hail Mary and One Glory be - may they know I silently pray always, I just do not have the words right now.

This isn’t about scrupulosity. It’s about learning to see my real life – work, mess, parish drama, doom scrolling - everything - – in the light of Christ.

Returning / struggling?

For those who feel late, lost, hurt or unsure if they still belong.

If you’re reading this and you feel guilty, angry, ashamed, unsure, or simply numb: you are exactly the kind of person this project is for. The Church is not a club for the sorted. It is a home for people who are still very much in process of fixing life - trust me I know.

This page will grow into a gentle, practical guide for coming back: how to approach confession, how to find a parish, what to do if you’re LGBTQ, what to do if you’ve been hurt.
