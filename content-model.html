Governance

Directory rulebook

This page sets out how listings are chosen, checked, corrected, and removed so the site stays useful, modest, and accountable.

Home / Directory rulebook

The Catholic Experiment is meant to be a Catholic gateway and directory, not a substitute for the parish, diocese, bishop, confessor, canon lawyer, or safeguarding office. Where possible it points outward to official Catholic routes rather than duplicating them.

The working standard is simple: direct first, summarise only where necessary, and never present private judgement as if it were an official Catholic source.

What belongs here

  • Official Catholic sources from the Holy See, bishops’ conferences, dioceses, parishes, shrines, religious houses, and recognised Catholic bodies.
  • Catholic businesses, publishers, and services that are publicly Catholic in identity and useful to Catholics in Britain, Ireland, and the islands.
  • Pilgrimage, retreat, and devotional places that people can genuinely visit or use.
  • Seeker and formation routes that point people onward to trusted official or well-grounded Catholic resources rather than inventing a parallel teaching authority.

What does not belong here

  • Private pastoral advice, unofficial commentary, or moral guidance presented as if it were official Church teaching.
  • Unverified parish copies, scraped listings, or stale directory mirrors.
  • Resources that claim Catholic authority without a clear source or ecclesial home.
  • Listings that raise safeguarding, reputational, or doctrinal doubts that cannot be resolved.

Public listing language

  • Official Church: outward links to the Holy See, bishops' conferences, dioceses, parishes, shrines, schools, or other Catholic bodies speaking in their own name.
  • Trusted Catholic: a Catholic organisation, publisher, house, or apostolate with a clear Catholic identity and a legitimate reason to be listed.
  • Listed for reference: a practical listing that may be useful to Catholics but is not being presented as an official ecclesial route or endorsement.

Source order

  1. Holy See and Vatican sources.
  2. Bishops’ conferences and diocesan sources.
  3. Official parish, shrine, monastery, school, or organisation websites.
  4. Reliable Catholic institutions where no better primary source is available.

If a better primary source is found later, the listing should be updated to use it.

Working principle

When there is a choice between being impressive and being reliable, reliability comes first.

Responsible publisher

Current public responsibility sits with Alan Gallagher of Waylight Atlantic. Any future ecclesial review will be sought through the Diocese of Clifton.

In doubt?

If a listing seems doubtful, broken, unsafe, or misleading, it should be flagged and checked rather than defended.

Trust labels

Trusted

Recorded from an official source or a clearly identifiable Catholic organisation, with a live outward link and a current reason to be listed.

Trust labels

Provisional or review

Held back or flagged when the source is incomplete, a link needs checking, or the Catholic identity or current activity cannot yet be confirmed.

Correction route

How updates happen

Corrections should come with an official URL wherever possible. If a listing changes materially, the old link should be replaced rather than layered over.

Removal

When something is taken down

A listing should be removed or hidden if it becomes unsafe, clearly inaccurate, no longer active, or impossible to verify in a responsible way.

Catholic governance and approval

The site is being prepared so it can later be shown to a priest and, where appropriate, diocesan authority for review. Until then it should avoid making claims it cannot support and should remain careful about the use of Catholic identity, official approval, and public trust.

Nothing in the public build should imply diocesan, episcopal, or Vatican endorsement unless that endorsement has genuinely been granted and published as such.

Where official Church documents help clarify a matter, the site should point directly to them. Useful reference points include the Code of Canon Law, the Catechism of the Catholic Church, the Catholic Bishops’ Conference of England and Wales, the Irish Catholic Bishops’ Conference, and the Bishops’ Conference of Scotland.

Practical validation workflow

  1. Find the best primary source available and record it.
  2. Check that the link is live, current, and clearly connected to the Catholic body being listed.
  3. Confirm the place, scope, and category are described plainly.
  4. Mark the record trusted only when its source and identity are clear.
  5. Invite corrections from visitors, clergy, religious, and local Catholics when something looks wrong.
  6. Review doubtful records promptly and remove them if clarity cannot be reached.

If you see something doubtful, please use the contact page and send the official source that should replace it.
