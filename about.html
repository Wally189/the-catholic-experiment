About the project

About The Catholic Experiment

A calm Catholic guide to parish life, trusted resources, and practical next steps across Britain and Ireland.

Home / About

The Catholic Experiment exists to make Catholic life easier to find and easier to begin in ordinary life across England, Wales, Scotland, Ireland, Northern Ireland, Orkney, the Isle of Man, and the Channel Islands / Jersey. It is place-first by design: start where you are, find a trustworthy local route, and then deepen prayer, formation, work, household life, and service in a steady way.

The project keeps a simple standard: point people toward reliable Catholic sources, avoid noise, and make next steps clearer. It is not here to replace parish life, diocesan guidance, or any official Catholic teaching office, but to help people reach them faster and with less confusion.

How to use this site

  1. Start with Catholic Britain & Ireland if you want a place-based beginning.
  2. Use Find a Parish and Find a Diocese when you need the nearest official Catholic route.
  3. Use Tools, Catholic Media, and Business Directory for practical Catholic reference points and outward links you can return to.

Where details are uncertain, they are held for manual verification rather than presented with false confidence. Trust comes first.

How the project is governed

This site is being shaped as a directory and gateway, not as a substitute teaching office. Its working standards are now published openly in the Directory rulebook, and its public boundaries are set out in the Safeguarding page.

The public build is currently managed by Alan Gallagher of Waylight Atlantic. Any future ecclesial review will be sought through the Diocese of Clifton. Until then, the site should be read as an independent Catholic directory project rather than as an official diocesan publication.

The intention is to stay modest: point outward to official Catholic sources, publish clear correction routes, and avoid public tools that are not yet ready for a careful Catholic launch.
