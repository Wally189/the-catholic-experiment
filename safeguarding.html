Safeguarding

Safeguarding and boundaries

This site exists to point people toward trustworthy Catholic routes. It is not a private ministry channel, counselling service, or substitute for formal safeguarding processes.

Home / Safeguarding

If there is an urgent risk of harm, contact the police or emergency services first. If the concern involves a parish, diocese, school, religious house, or Catholic organisation, use the official safeguarding route for that body as well.

This public build is currently operated by Alan Gallagher of Waylight Atlantic. Any future ecclesial review of the project will be sought through the Diocese of Clifton, but safeguarding concerns themselves must always go through the proper civil and Church reporting routes.

What this site will and will not do

  • It will point toward official Catholic contact routes, dioceses, and trusted organisations.
  • It will accept correction notices about broken links, doubtful listings, or concerns about whether something should remain listed.
  • It will not offer private spiritual direction, crisis response, counselling, confession, or informal safeguarding handling through casual messages.
  • It will not publish public commitments, witness rolls, or browser-based moderation tools until proper governance and safeguarding structures exist.

Official safeguarding routes

  • Catholic Safeguarding Standards Agency for England and Wales.
  • National Board for Safeguarding Children in the Catholic Church in Ireland for Ireland.
  • Bishops’ Conference of Scotland as a national starting point for Scottish diocesan safeguarding routes.

Contact expectations

  • Do not send sensitive personal disclosures through a public website form.
  • Use official diocesan, parish, or safeguarding channels when a matter belongs there.
  • If a listed resource looks unsafe, misleading, or improperly represented, send the official source and explain the concern.

Directory safeguard

A directory can still do harm if it keeps broken, misleading, or unsafe routes alive. That is why doubtful listings should be checked quickly and removed when necessary.

Need to report something here?

Use the contact page for listing concerns. For emergencies or safeguarding reports, go directly to the proper civil or Church authority. The wider public boundaries for the site are also set out in the Directory rulebook.

Children, vulnerable adults, and privacy

The Catholic Experiment should never invite children or vulnerable adults into informal direct contact without clear safeguarding structures, named responsibility, and appropriate data handling. For that reason, public sign-up features and browser-based moderation tools have been withdrawn from the public build.

Any future communication tools should be minimal, consent-based, and tied to a real backend with access control, audit logging, and a clear privacy policy.
