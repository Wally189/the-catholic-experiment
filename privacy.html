Privacy Policy

This policy explains how personal data is handled in the current public build of The Catholic Experiment.

Home / Privacy Policy

The public build of this site is intentionally minimal. It mainly publishes pages, external links, and structured directory data. Contact is email-first, public sign-up features are paused, and browser-based moderation tools are not part of the live public experience.

What data may be handled

  • If you contact the site by email, your message and contact details will be used only to reply, review a correction, or handle the question you have raised.
  • The site uses local storage for basic preferences such as theme choice, language toggle state, and cookie choices.
  • Standard hosting or server logs may record technical request data needed for security and delivery.

What is not currently active

  • No public witness roll.
  • No public browser-based moderation panel.
  • No third-party form relay in the public build.
  • No public mailing-list sign-up form until a fuller backend and governance model are in place.

How data is used and retained

  • Correspondence is used only for the purpose for which it was sent.
  • Correction and safeguarding-related messages may be kept for as long as reasonably necessary to resolve the matter responsibly.
  • No personal data is sold or knowingly shared for marketing purposes.
  • You can request access, correction, or deletion by contacting the site contact address the published site contact route once it is configured.
