New to the Faith

What happens at Mass?

A simple guide for those who want to attend a Catholic parish quietly and understand the broad shape of the liturgy.

Home / New to the faith / What happens at Mass?

If you visit Mass for the first time, it may feel unfamiliar. That is normal. You are not expected to know every response, gesture, or custom at once.

The most useful first approach is simple: arrive, sit quietly, observe, listen, and let the shape of the liturgy become familiar over time.

You can simply attend

  • You may sit near the back if that helps you settle.
  • You do not need to say or sing everything straight away.
  • You can follow the congregation as best you can and remain seated when unsure.
Broad shape

The liturgy of the Word

The first main part of Mass includes readings from Scripture, a Psalm, the Gospel, a homily, prayers of intercession, and often the Creed on Sundays and solemnities.

This part of the Mass is about hearing the word of God proclaimed and preached within the prayer of the Church.

Broad shape

The liturgy of the Eucharist

The second main part includes the preparation of the altar, the Eucharistic Prayer, the Our Father, Holy Communion, and the final dismissal.

For Catholics, this is the heart of the Mass: the Church's sacrificial and sacramental worship in union with Christ.

What may feel unfamiliar

Standing, sitting, kneeling, silence

Mass includes bodily prayer as well as spoken prayer. People stand, sit, kneel, bow, make the Sign of the Cross, and keep moments of silence. If you are unsure, it is fine to follow gently or simply remain still.

If you are not Catholic

Holy Communion and participation

If you are not Catholic, you would normally remain in your place during Holy Communion. That is not a rejection. It reflects the Catholic understanding that sacramental communion belongs within full communion with the Church.

You are still welcome to attend, pray quietly, and stay for the whole Mass.

A practical approach

How to begin without pressure

  1. Attend Mass a few times rather than trying to judge it from one visit.
  2. Notice the rhythm of readings, prayer, silence, and reverence.
  3. Use a missal or readings app later if it helps, but do not feel you must manage everything on day one.
  4. When ready, ask a priest or parishioner about anything that still seems unclear.
