New to the Catholic faith

A gentle starting point for those who are curious, returning, asking about OCIA, or trying to understand Catholic life for the first time.

Home / New to the Catholic faith

You do not need to understand everything before you begin. Start simply: learn who Jesus is, visit Sunday Mass, ask questions honestly, and let your local parish help you take the next step.

This page is for seekers, inquirers, and anyone asking whether Catholic life might be for them. It is also for those who have drifted away and want a calm way back.

A gentle first step

  • Read one Gospel first, especially Mark or Luke.
  • Visit Mass without pressure to understand every detail immediately.
  • Speak to a parish about OCIA if you want to explore seriously.
  • Keep prayer simple and steady rather than dramatic.
Start here

What is OCIA?

OCIA, the Order of Christian Initiation of Adults, is the ordinary parish route for adults who want to explore becoming Catholic, or who need baptism, confirmation, or a fuller reception into the Church.

You do not need to be certain before making contact. A parish can help you enquire before any formal commitment is made.

First visit

What to expect at Mass

You are welcome to attend Mass and observe. You can sit quietly, follow along as best you can, and simply notice the shape of Catholic prayer.

If you are not Catholic you would normally remain in your place during Holy Communion, but you are welcome to stay for the whole Mass.

Read

Begin with one Gospel, then the Creed, then selected parts of the Catechism rather than trying to absorb everything at once.

A simple first month

A realistic way to begin

  1. Read ten minutes of a Gospel each day.
  2. Attend Sunday Mass for a few weeks.
  3. Learn the Our Father and Hail Mary.
  4. Contact one parish and ask about OCIA or enquiry meetings.
  5. Keep notes on the questions that keep returning.

Questions worth asking

  • What do Catholics believe about Jesus and the sacraments?
  • How does confession work?
  • Why do Catholics honour Mary and the saints?
  • What is the difference between private devotion and public worship?
  • How do I begin if I have little or no religious background?
When you are ready

Contacting a parish

If you are wondering what to say, keep it simple: “I’m exploring the Catholic faith and would like to ask about OCIA or an enquiry conversation.” That is enough.

A good parish will understand that people arrive with different histories, questions, and levels of certainty.
