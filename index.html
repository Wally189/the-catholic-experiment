Failte - Welcome.

This is The Catholic Experiment. You are most welcome....

I've been thinking about creating a platform like this for a long time. I am a layman, actually a relatively new Catholic - I was only confirmed and baptised at the Easter Service in 2024. Yet I have been attending Mass and involved to some degree or other in the church my whole life. I will not lie, it has been a long journey which I am sure we will go into in due course; and the journey continues.

The project is here to document the journey. I made a vow to God that I would dedicate my life to him when I finally made the commitment to become Catholic. It was an easy decision in the sense that God exists. I have known that since I can remember - although I did deliberately question it for a long time. It was a difficult decision in the sense that I am human. I am full of sin and I carry baggage that often makes me feel separate from the wider church. That is not a reflection on the church - it's a reflection on me and my own anxieties.

I often feel I do not fit in traditional groups, for whatever reasons but I do feel the Church (capital C!) can guide me so that I can live a good and Catholic life. In secular England too! Come with me on that journey....

Le meas,

Alan

You do not need to open every door. Just the one you need today.

Start here

A simple welcome for anyone who is interested in Catholicism wherever you are in the world. This is a single-person project that focuses on the basics: prayer, Scripture, Mass, Confession, and the slow work of living like a Catholic in the modern world.

  • The seed of an idea for this 'project' was the sheer lack of resources out there for English Catholics - I wanted to add a non-clerical dimension with a uniquely English / Irish perspective.
  • I also wanted to share my journey. I am a sinner - a BIG one. That is not said in a Prideful way but if I can turn the corner, perhaps it will inspire others
  • Over the years I have developed Catholic practices based on traditional routines and rythms and adapted them to modern life. This is a, hopefully, plain and practical companion to help people along the way
