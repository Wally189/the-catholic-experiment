Support

How support is used

Optional support exists to sustain the directory, not to compromise it.

Home / Support / How support is used

If people support this project, the money should serve the mission plainly: running costs, verification work, accessibility improvements, and the steady labour of keeping the directory accurate and usable.

No hidden influence: optional support does not buy ecclesial endorsement, softer moderation, hidden placement, or private control over listings.

What support may cover

  • Hosting, domain, backups, and ordinary site maintenance.
  • Directory verification, link checking, and correction handling.
  • Accessibility, usability, and mobile improvements.
  • Careful expansion of diocesan, parish, retreat, business, and resource coverage.

What support does not mean

  • It does not create official Church status.
  • It does not turn a listing into a diocesan or Vatican recommendation.
  • It does not remove the public standards in the Directory rulebook.
  • It does not override the Safeguarding page or the legal pages.

Public review

The current public build is operated by Alan Gallagher of Waylight Atlantic. The intended ecclesial review path is through the Diocese of Clifton. Support therefore has to sit under clear public standards rather than informal private promises.
