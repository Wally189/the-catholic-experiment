About the project

About the Experiment

A central Catholic resource for the UK & Ireland, born from a personal project and the sense that Catholic life across these islands needs a more joined-up, beautiful, practical public face.

Home / About

The Catholic Experiment began as a personal project after seeing how fragmented Catholic life can feel online across England, Wales, Scotland, Ireland, Northern Ireland, the Channel Islands, and the Isle of Man. Some sites are excellent but isolated; some are functional but lifeless; many ordinary Catholics do not easily see how prayer, work, parish, household order, charity, formation, and rest belong together.

The project responds to that gap. It tries to make Catholic life intelligible again in public: beautiful without sentimentality, practical without reducing everything to technique, and rooted in the actual rhythms of these islands.

It also takes seriously the de-Christianisation of Britain and Ireland. Many people have inherited fragments, clichés, or suspicions rather than a living picture of the faith. This site tries, modestly, to remove the veil: to show what Catholic life is really about in practice, and to direct people to the trustworthy places where the Church is still alive.
