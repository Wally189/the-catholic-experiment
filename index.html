Schedule

Only one Experiment is developed intensively at a time. The programme currently prioritises platform stabilisation, then the Latin pilot, before further Faith production.

Current sequence: common platform → Latin pilot definition → genuine study and delivery → evaluation → decision on the next intensive Experiment.

Materials

The book remains central. The aim is to make serious study possible with simple physical materials and lawful access to the relevant sources.

Practical

What you need to study

Keep the essential kit modest. Anything beyond the required items is optional.

Required

  • One ruled notebook or exercise book
  • Pen
  • Pencil and eraser
  • Access to the course text

Recommended

  • Ruler for clear page layout
  • Dictionary appropriate to the subject
  • Headphones for listening work

Optional

  • Flashcards
  • Separate vocabulary notebook
  • Printer or loose-leaf folder

Transparency and editorial standards

Sources
Older books are labelled as historical learning spines. Current official, academic or technical sources are identified separately as controlling or validating sources.
Limitations
This is an independent learning project, not a university course, professional qualification or substitute for qualified pastoral, academic, legal or technical advice.
Corrections
Factual, doctrinal, accessibility and source corrections are welcomed through the form above. Material corrections will be reviewed and recorded.
Version control
Public pages display a version and review date. Significant changes are retained in the project’s internal release and evaluation records.
Ecclesiastical status
The project does not claim an imprimatur, nihil obstat or other ecclesiastical approval unless such approval is expressly obtained and identified.

The Catholic Experiment is an independent learning project. It does not imply ecclesiastical approval.
