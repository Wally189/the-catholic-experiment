Faith & Formation

Study the faith

A steady route through the Gospels, the Creed, the Mass, prayer, and the broad shape of Catholic teaching.

Home / Study the faith

When people begin to study the Catholic faith, the main problem is often not lack of material but too much of it. The better approach is to learn in an order that is humanly manageable and ecclesially trustworthy.

Start with the Gospel, then the Creed, then the sacraments, then the shape of Catholic moral and spiritual life. Let parish worship and prayer accompany that reading from the beginning.

A simple order

  • Read one Gospel before trying to solve every controversy.
  • Learn the Creed as the map of the faith.
  • See doctrine in the life of the Church, not only on a page.
  • Use official or well-grounded sources when questions become complex.

A reading path

One workable order for a beginner

  1. Read Mark or Luke from beginning to end.
  2. Learn the Creed and use it as a map.
  3. Visit Mass and notice how belief is prayed.
  4. Read selected parts of the Catechism with patience rather than speed.
  5. Return to prayer and parish life while questions continue to deepen.
