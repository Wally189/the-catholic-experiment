Home / Accessibility Statement

This accessibility statement sets out the current measures in place, recognised limitations, and a clear route for feedback. It also notes dark mode, keyboard navigation, reduced motion handling, and semantic structure.

Because the work serves readers across Britain and Ireland, the wording is kept clear and careful, with room for further refinement as the site grows in use and depth.

Current accessibility features

  • Skip link and semantic landmarks
  • Keyboard-friendly navigation
  • Dark mode and reduced motion support
  • Readable contrast and scalable text

Accessibility feedback

Accessibility feedback can be sent to the site contact address.

A direct accessibility contact route will appear here once the site inbox is configured.

Further practical work

  • Keep contact details current and easy to find.
  • List the processors and hosting providers that support the work.
  • Review and confirm retention periods.
  • Review legal wording regularly so it remains accurate in each jurisdiction served.
