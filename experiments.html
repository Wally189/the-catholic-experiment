Experiments

Try the Catholic life, one act at a time

These aren’t self-improvement hacks. They’re small, realistic, honest attempts to live the Catholic way of life in ordinary circumstances: work, fatigue, bills, loneliness, bad weeks, parish drama and everything in between.

Pick one. Do it imperfectly. Make notes. Let God do the quiet work.

“Experiments help me avoid vague good intentions. I try something small, on purpose, for a defined time — and I watch what God does.”

How the Experiments work

The structure is simple on purpose.

1. Choose one practice

Each experiment focuses on a single Catholic practice — prayer, devotion, sacrament, rhythm or habit. Nothing overwhelming.

2. Commit for a short period

Usually 7–14 days, sometimes 30. Long enough to notice something, short enough to try again if you fail.

3. Reflect, don’t score yourself

No success metrics. Just reflection: What changed? What resisted? What surprised me? Where was God?
