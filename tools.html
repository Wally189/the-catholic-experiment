Catholic tools

A true Catholic tool deck

Missals, breviaries, prayer apps, catechisms, Bibles, and reference sites that help Catholic life become steady, usable, and practical.

Home / Catholic Tools

Think of this page as a working shelf. Some tools belong in the hand, some on the phone, some on the desk, and some in the parish bag you take to Mass.

The point is not to own everything. The point is to find the few tools that genuinely help you pray, learn, and stay close to the Church.

Where to begin

  • Choose one good Bible and one Catechism.
  • Use a missal or readings app for Sunday Mass.
  • If the Hours call to you, begin with Compline or Morning Prayer.
  • Buy from Catholic bookshops and local suppliers where possible.

Missals

A missal helps you follow the prayers and readings of Mass. A Sunday missal is usually the simplest place to begin if daily Mass is not yet part of your routine.

Find Catholic booksellers

Liturgy of the Hours

You do not need the full multi-volume set on day one. Many people begin with a one-volume breviary, a simple daily office book, or an app such as Universalis.

Prayer helps

A rosary, a small prayer book, a notebook, and a quiet routine often do more spiritual good than an overfilled shelf.

Open the prayer library

Practical guidance

How to choose tools well

  • Choose tools that help you return regularly, not merely tools that impress you.
  • For study, prefer official texts and trusted Catholic publishers.
  • For prayer, simplicity is usually stronger than novelty.
  • For children or beginners, use clear editions and gentle introductions.

Buying wisely

If you are buying a missal, breviary, Bible, rosary, or devotional book, try Catholic shops and publishers first. The business directory is meant to make that easier.
