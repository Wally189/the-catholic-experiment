Roadmap

How this will grow

The next stage is not to become louder. It is to become more accurate, more accountable, and more clearly directory-led.

Home / How this will grow

The site is strongest when it helps people find the right Catholic route quickly and confidently. Growth therefore has to mean better coverage, clearer standards, and more visible accountability.

Near-term priorities

  • Reduce legacy and placeholder residue across older pages.
  • Keep raw page wording aligned with the public site wording.
  • Expand directory coverage without pretending to be exhaustive before the evidence is there.
  • Clarify trust levels: official Church, trusted Catholic, listed for reference.

Public accountability

The current public build is operated by Alan Gallagher of Waylight Atlantic. The intended ecclesial review path is through the Diocese of Clifton. Until explicit approval is granted, the site should remain careful in tone, modest in claims, and transparent about its status.

What growth should not mean

  • Not more inward commentary when a good official source already exists.
  • Not more opinionated moral writing when careful reference links would serve better.
  • Not commercial clutter or manipulative fundraising.
  • Not a private substitute for parish, diocesan, or safeguarding structures.
