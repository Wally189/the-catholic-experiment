Welcome to The Catholic Experiment

A Catholic resource for Britain and Ireland

The Catholic Experiment is a practical Catholic home for Britain and Ireland, and for anyone quietly exploring the faith. It gathers places, resources, and guidance that help people find Catholic life nearby and take a steady next step.

The site is organised around six pillars: prayer and formation, vocation and work, the life of the home, community and parish, health and preparedness, and rest and renewal. The aim is simple: point people toward trustworthy Catholic sources and present them clearly, calmly, and without noise.

Many excellent Catholic resources already exist, but they are often scattered or hard to find if you are looking for something local. This project gathers what is useful for Catholics living in these islands and makes it easier to return to.

Whether you are practising, returning, curious, or simply trying to find reliable answers, you are welcome here.

Explore Catholic Britain & Ireland

The ancient faith of these islands, living again in public

Catholicism is not an imported novelty here. It is among the ancient roots of these islands: the faith of saints, monasteries, shrines, pilgrims, scholars, martyrs, and ordinary households long before later ruptures and re-orderings in public life.

Though much of Britain and Ireland passed through centuries of division, forgetting, and de-Christianisation, the Catholic faith was never extinguished. In parishes, schools, families, religious houses, pilgrim places, and new converts, it continues to take root again. This section helps map where that life already exists.

Use the site to find parishes, dioceses, tools, pilgrimage places, charities, businesses, retreat centres, and study paths. The six pillars explain how Catholic life is lived; Explore helps you find where that life is still being renewed.
