Life & dignity

Official Catholic resources on the dignity of life from conception to natural death, and the care owed to every person.

Home / Home & Family / Life & dignity

The Catholic witness on life is not only a matter of public controversy. It is also about accompaniment, disability, old age, illness, pregnancy, conscience, grief, medical ethics, and the refusal to treat any person as a burden or a problem to be solved. Official resources matter here because the stakes are high and because slogans are not enough.

The resources below offer a steadier place to begin if you want to understand the Church's view, follow current bishops' conference guidance, or think more seriously about care at the end of life.

Begin with dignity: the Church's public witness on life makes more sense when read together with her insistence on mercy, accompaniment, and care for the vulnerable.
