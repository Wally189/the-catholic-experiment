Business Directory

Directory policy

The public standards for what belongs here, how listings are classified, and how stronger claims are handled.

Home / Business Directory / Directory policy

This directory is meant to be useful without becoming vague or inflated. The word Catholic carries ecclesial implications, so the basis for inclusion should be stated clearly and the limits of each listing should remain visible.

What may be listed

  • Businesses owned or run by Catholics.
  • Businesses serving Catholic life.
  • Businesses offering goods or services relevant to Catholic practice.
  • Ecclesially referenced entries, but only where the stronger connection can actually be evidenced.

What a listing does not mean

  • Not endorsement by a parish.
  • Not endorsement by a diocese.
  • Not endorsement by the bishops' conference.
  • Not endorsement by the Holy See.

Primary listing types

  • Catholic-owned: the business is owned or run by Catholics and that is part of the reason for inclusion.
  • Catholic-serving: the business serves Catholic life but is not being presented primarily on the basis of ownership.
  • Catholic goods/services: the goods or services are directly relevant to Catholic practice.
  • Ecclesially referenced: a stronger Church-linked connection is documented and stated plainly.

Verification levels

  • Standard listing: basic listing level with no stronger claim.
  • Editorially reviewed: checked by the site editor against public evidence.
  • Priest referenced: a priest reference has been documented.
  • Diocesan reference: a diocesan reference has been documented.

Guardrails

  • Listings must not promote activity clearly contrary to Catholic moral teaching.
  • Listings must not falsely claim Church endorsement.
  • Listings must not be misleading or fraudulent.
  • Listings must not damage the credibility of the directory.

The current public build is operated by Alan Gallagher of Waylight Atlantic. Any future ecclesial review will be sought through the Diocese of Clifton.
