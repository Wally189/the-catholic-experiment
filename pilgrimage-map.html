Pilgrimage

Pilgrimage in Britain & Ireland

Discover Catholic shrines, ancient holy places, and simple ways to make pilgrimage across these islands or close to home.

Start here

Go to a shrine, or begin where you are

Pilgrimage is one of the old Christian ways of praying with the whole body. Some people can travel to places like Walsingham or Knock. Others need to begin with a parish church, a local walk, a holy well, or a quiet day of prayer at home. Both can be real acts of devotion.

This page gathers a few of the best-known Catholic shrines and older holy places across the UK and Ireland, with a simple invitation: go prayerfully, go honestly, and let the journey make room for God.

Major shrines

Catholic places of pilgrimage

England

Walsingham

The national Catholic shrine of Our Lady in Norfolk, with the Slipper Chapel, pilgrim programme, and long English tradition of Marian devotion.

St Winefride's Well

Holywell remains one of the most striking Catholic pilgrimage places in Britain, with a living tradition of prayer, healing, and devotion.

Ancient holy places

Older places that still draw pilgrims

Not every holy place is a Catholic shrine in the same sense. Some are older monastic and sacred sites that still reward a reverent visit, especially if you arrive with silence, Scripture, and prayer.

Scotland

Iona

The island of St Columba remains one of the great Christian landscapes of these islands, with abbey ruins, burial ground, and long memory of prayer.

Learn more

England

Glastonbury

Glastonbury holds layers of Christian memory, and the Catholic Shrine of Our Lady keeps that Marian and diocesan pilgrimage life visible today.

Learn more

England

Lindisfarne

Holy Island is bound up with St Aidan, St Cuthbert, and the missionary life of the early Church in Northumbria.

Learn more

If you want walking routes and broader ancient-site inspiration, the British Pilgrimage Trust is especially helpful for places like Iona, Glastonbury, and Lindisfarne.

Pilgrim at home

Make a small pilgrimage without travelling far

You do not need to wait for a long journey. A local pilgrimage can be as simple as setting out on foot to a parish church, a quiet chapel, a cemetery, or a place where you can pray well.

  • Choose an intention before you leave the house.
  • Walk part of the route in silence and keep your phone away.
  • Pray one mystery of the Rosary, a Psalm, or the Angelus on the way.
  • End with Mass, Adoration, a holy hour, or a simple thanksgiving before an icon or crucifix at home.

A gentle pattern

  • Set out with a clear intention.
  • Take one reading from the Gospels or from a local saint.
  • Carry a small cross, rosary, or pilgrim prayer.
  • Let the journey be slower than an ordinary errand.
